Playing John Muir

Playing John Muir was a toolbox created from sheet metal and leather to store nature's most precious treasures. The box was modeled after a geode and it's pockets inspired by plants and organic matter. We wanted to create something that was not only functional, but was also an experience that related to the things the box would hold.

Details
This project was created in 2011 at UCLA
Goals
Design and make a toolbox out of sheet metal.
Creative Process

Process

The box was conceptualized and modeled in Rhino 3D. The exterior and interior were separated and flattened to become stencils. Materials were then cut and combined to make the finished product.

Just like a rock can be both sharp and soft, we wanted to manipulate the naturally hard surface of sheet metal to appear soft. The leather "stems" that connected the pockets were stuffed with padding to look like organs from a body. The lazer-cut lattice from the pockets had the effect of walking through the dappled sunlight of a rain forest within the box.
