About XYZ

Background

In my time at XYZ, I’ve had the opportunity to work on a variety of complex products, from a partner database to an online search tool. One significant challenge of working here has been balancing the deep set traditions of the company with the value of modern design, especially with older users of 50+ years who may not always embrace new technology or complex flows; however, I have found this to present good design challenges. Working with the users at XYZ has allowed me to identify and compensate for my own biases, remain objective, seek deeper user research, and design with accessibility in mind.

I believe that all design should consider accessibility. As technology gets more and more complex, it’s the job of the designer to translate those complexities into something simple for the user. It’s easy to get carried away with a problem and land on solutions that seem creative and unique, but have no use to the user because of it’s complexity. When we design for accessibility, it forces us to remember to design for the user and not ourselves. It forces us to find the most simple solutions.

There was a dichotomy between engineers and design when I first joined, so I established unifying design principles for us to focus our work. They included the following:

Accessibility

We design for an audience that has difficulty using online tools and have aging eyes. We want welcome these users into a domain they are usually uncomfortable with. We do this with larger targets such as form fields, buttons, and cards.

Simple

We remove color and distractions and enforce consistency so that attention will remain focused on content. We keep all interactions as straight forward as possible and have a bias toward explicit versus implicit interactions.

Effortless

We always try to think ahead of the user. We preemptively know what questions they will ask at each step of the way and design for that. We tailor copy and design to help with making the user feel like the entire process is seamless.

Optimized for Google

We need to let people know that we exist and that we are here to help them. We put the most important content ATF, use the right heirarchy of headings, and add a descriptive page title and meta text.
