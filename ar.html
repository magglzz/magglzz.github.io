Augmented Reality

I'm curious about the future and how design plays a role in it, so I've been using my Architecture training to dabble in unity. This is a project that I created as a fun experiment to learn Unity and familiarize myself with designing in the XR space.

Details
This project was created in December 2017
Catching Kittens

Planning

This game using simple unity principles of detecting a plane, having objects come into the environment, and then collecting those objects before they are destroyed by hitting the plane.

Before the final product, I worked on different objectives in Unity. Some of them included placing objects into the environment without using plane detection and using different "collection" methods such as shooting and moving a basket. The simplest and easiest to understand for the user with my limited Unity knowledge was tapping the objects to collect them.

Catching Kittens

The objective of the game is to tap the falling kittens before they hit the plane. You start off with a health score of 40 and for each kitten that touches the plane, they are destroyed and you lose 2 health points. If you tap a kitten to catch it, you gain 1 score point. The game is over once you reach 0 health points.

Learnings

Usability in the AR space is world's different than the digital 2D space that I am accustomed to. The designer must take into account how the program is used by the body. Does the user have to move around a lot? Do they have to keep their arms extended for long periods of time? One particular issue that consistently comes up in AR is how to portray to the user the finicky bits of AR that they may not be aware of. For example, you need lots of light and contrast for your app to work. You also need to move around so that the program can detect your environment and build information on it. These are just the tip of the iceberg for the design challenges that designers in the AR space face.
