Search

With our current search feature, it’s hard for our users to find senior care. There are so many options and steps involved that most users drop off the funnel early on in the process. They feel overwhelmed and confused by the options. In order to improve the search experience, we decided to revamp the entire search flow.

My Role
As lead designer, I helped product managers conduct user research to find pain points and blockers for achieving our goals. I also went through multiple iterations of prototype creation, conducting user tests as well as creating the final responsive design.
Goals
To significantly increase engagement, qualitative usability, and conversion on the search results pages.
Research

Problems

The original spec outlined the following problems:

  • Current search results do not provide enough information about the communities to decide which ones are interesting.
  • Current search results give the user too many choices to evaluate and no method of trimming them down
  • Users don’t always have a great idea of what level of care they need

We used the following points to define what would make a better search experience:

  • User gets results without sense of mental exertion
  • User has a list of communities that they want to explore beyond the website

01

Flow Mapping

The beginning of the search flow starts at the homepage. However with the current homepage design, the user has no idea how to enter the flow. The hierarchy of content on the homepage tells the story of a website full of articles and information about caregiving, but not about searching for senior care. Our search tool doesn’t even seem to exist. To fix this, I decided to tie a redesign of the homepage directly with the redesign of our search.

02

Homepage

Caring.com is a hub of information and resources for caregivers, seniors, and anyone looking for senior care. At the same time, we are referral service with a full directory of senior living communities and agencies. I split the screen in half to show the dual nature of our website. The left side would stay static with the search feature always accessible, while the right side would scroll and allow the user to browse our content. The team chose to show the quick links first in order to immediately answer the 6 most common questions consumers have when they visit our website.

The goal was to significantly increase engagement, qualitative usability, and conversion on the search results pages.

03

Search

With the new design, the user is taken directly from the homepage to an easy to understand search results page, skipping the 4 intermediary steps that our old design had. The new search page would include a map and a list and follow common design patterns that our users would be more familiar navigating.

04

Conclusion

This project is in the process of being built as of October 2017.
